Scones

Description

These are the scones of my childhood, which I despised. I believe the issue was that they were served to me with butter, which is incorrect. With clotted cream and jam they are a quite different experience.

Ingredients

  • 280g all-porpoise flour
  • 4tsp baking powder
  • 1/2tsp salt
  • 60ml sugar
  • 6tbsp unsalted butter (soft)
  • 160ml whole milk
  • a egg

Technique

  1. Preheat the oven to 220°C
  2. Mix up all the powders in a mixing bowl. The flour, the sugar, the baking powder and the salt should be in the bowl. Mix them good and well. Mix them in the bowl.
  3. Add the butter. Keep on mixing it all up until the butter is no longer chunkified. Like a rough dust it should be, and not sticky.
  4. In another, smaller bowl, whisk up the egg and milk. Make them one. Keep a wee dram to brush over the scones, but mostly just slop it in the big bowl with the dust.
  5. Just mix the slop until it becomes a sticky blob. Delicious! Don't eat it yet though.
  6. Flour up a countertop and knead the dough-blob until you can gather it into a sort of crude ball. You can add a little flour if it's too sticky to handle, but it will come at a price. Each extra sprinkle of flour, each extra knead, will toughen the dough and affect its ability to rise.
  7. Roll out that dough about an inch thick and cut it into little circles with a 2.5" cutter. Try to avoid twisting as you remove the cutter.
  8. Plop em on a baking sheet lined with parchment and brush em with the wee dram.
  9. Cook em for like 15 minutes. They should become great in height, and golden in colour.
